24-hour Clock

Generally, in French, you divide the entire day into 24 hours.

In English, the day is usually referred to as two sections of 12 hours each (a.m and p.m.) before and after noon (12 p.m.).

However, in French, the time is treize heures after midi. And vingt-trois heures just before minuit.
